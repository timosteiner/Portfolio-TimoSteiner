UNIVERSITY
BACKGROUND

A mathematician works with numbers.
A psychologist works with people.
But what does a media engineer work with?

I added this page about the university background for two reasons.

First, I want to give you a clearer understanding of what is realistically possible in potential freelance projects. That is why you will find a detailed overview of the study modules further down on this page ‚Äî they help to show which areas I have worked in and which skill sets could theoretically be applied to real projects.

Even more important, however, is to highlight how practice-oriented the program was. Many of our assignments were closely connected to real-world scenarios and often resembled actual freelance work. In the first semester, for example, we produced a portrait film about a professional football player while simultaneously developing a fictional startup from scratch. This included concept development, branding, communication strategy, and a final pitch. Even though the startup itself was fictional, the workflow and expectations were very realistic and provided valuable insight into entrepreneurial thinking.

In the second semester, we created a documentary-style film project about a drag queen. In parallel, we developed a TikTok concept in collaboration with the University of Applied Sciences of the Grisons ‚Äî a concept that was not only designed but also produced and published, meaning it was implemented and used in the real world.

During the third semester, we worked on a larger external collaboration. As a team, we spent an entire week in Berlin producing a documentary film about a youth center in Marzahn-Hellersdorf. The finished project was later broadcast on Alex Berlin TV, giving the project real public visibility beyond the university environment.

In the fourth semester, I became part of a real startup project. We designed 3D characters in Blender and built an online shop to sell them, combining creative production, technical execution, and business thinking into one hands-on workflow.

In the fifth semester, we collaborated with Theater Neumarkt, for whom we developed and implemented an Instagram concept. Projects like these reflect the core idea of the degree program: learning by doing, working with real partners, and creating results that function beyond an academic setting.
