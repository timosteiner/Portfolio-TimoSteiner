My first visit to Madikwe Game Reserve dates back to 2010, during a road trip through South Africa where my family and I explored the country from top to bottom. At that time, Madikwe was just one of many stops ‚Äî impressive, but still distant.

That changed significantly in 2013, when my father purchased a lodge in Madikwe. Since then, the reserve has become a constant part of my life. Over the years, I have returned roughly thirty times, across different seasons, light conditions, and wildlife cycles. This frequency has allowed me to build a deep understanding of the area ‚Äî not just visually, but rhythmically: how animals move, where light works best, and how the landscape changes throughout the day.

Madikwe Game Reserve is located in the far north of South Africa, right along the border to Botswana, close to Gaborone. Covering approximately 75,000 hectares ‚Äî roughly the size of more than 100,000 football fields ‚Äî it is one of South Africa‚Äôs largest privately managed game reserves. The reserve is home to the Big Five and is also malaria-free, making it unique in both its biodiversity and accessibility. Vast open plains, rocky hills, the Marico River, and dense bushland create a visually rich and constantly evolving environment. These diverse landscapes make Madikwe an exceptional place for wildlife photography, offering endless variations in composition, light and atmosphere.

Over time, Madikwe has shaped not only my photographic style, but also my way of observing nature. Knowing a place this well allows for patience, anticipation and respect ‚Äî qualities that are essential when telling authentic visual stories in the wild.
