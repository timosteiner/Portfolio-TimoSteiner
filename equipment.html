Cameras üì∑

The photography journey began with the Nikon D5600, an excellent entry-level camera that taught me the fundamentals of shooting and composition while staying within a reasonable budget. With its 24.2-megapixel APS-C sensor, it delivered sharp, vibrant images and an impressive dynamic range for its class. It was the perfect camera for beginners: reliable, user-friendly and capable.

After some time, however, I noticed that the D5600 was reaching its limits. Especially in low-light conditions, the APS-C sensor struggled, and in terms of video, I wanted more flexibility. All full-frame cameras that fit my needs at the time were in a much higher price range, particularly when considering my video requirements. So I decided to take an intermediate step and switched to the Canon EOS R7.

The R7 offered everything I was looking for in an advanced camera: 32.5 megapixels, Canon Log for professional video recording, and a fast autofocus system that tracks subjects with precision. Although it is also an APS-C camera, I was particularly impressed by its subject tracking, it reliably detects faces, animals and objects and keeps them in focus. Thanks to its in-body image stabilization (IBIS) and excellent 4K video quality, the R7 quickly became my preferred camera for photo and video work, especially in the wildlife field. It proved that an APS-C camera can deliver professional results without the weight or cost of a full-frame system.

As you can probably tell from my words, I am still very happy with the R7. However, it was clear to me that, due to the smaller sensor and my desire to pursue freelance collaborations even in low-light situations, an upgrade would eventually be necessary.

Today, I shoot most of the time with the Canon EOS R5 Mark II, which feels like the natural evolution of my setup ‚Äî essentially the R7, but on a completely new level. Its 45-megapixel full-frame sensor captures incredible detail, rich colors and delivers outstanding low-light performance. With 8K RAW video, oversampled 4K and an advanced intelligent autofocus system, it is a true powerhouse for professional work. Combined with robust weather sealing, dual card slots and extremely fast burst shooting (30 frames per second), it offers both reliability and creative freedom.

Looking back, each of these cameras represents an important milestone: The D5600 built the foundation, the R7 expanded my possibilities, and the R5 Mark II now gives me the tools to push my creativity as far as it can go.
